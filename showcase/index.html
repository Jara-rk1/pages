Recommendation

APPROVE the $2.4B social housing investment program to proceed to detailed business case, noting the positive economic case under central assumptions.
DIRECT staged delivery with independent cost assurance at each tranche to manage risk of construction cost overruns exceeding 27%.
DIRECT tenant allocation framework prioritising individuals experiencing or at immediate risk of homelessness to maximise social return.

$2.4B capital over 8 years + $102M p.a. recurrent at full delivery. Net State fiscal savings of $80–85M p.a. partially offset operating costs.

At the 7% central discount rate, the project can absorb up to 27% in construction cost overruns before NPV turns negative. This is a moderate safety margin.

Key Limitations

  • Health and justice savings rely on benefit transfer from AHURI studies — no primary valuation conducted
  • Consumer surplus estimate is indicative; a state-specific discrete choice experiment would improve precision
  • Analysis does not capture intergenerational benefits (children's educational outcomes), which would increase BCR
  • No allowance for crowding-out effects on private construction activity
  • Operating cost estimates may understate if maintenance backlog develops
  • Distributional analysis uses average tax incidence, not marginal rates
Upside not modelled: Intergenerational benefits, neighbourhood effects, and reduced child protection system costs are excluded. The true BCR is likely higher than estimated.
